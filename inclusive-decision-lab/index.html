About the Lab

Inclusive Decision Lab is a strategic accessibility governance simulation framework designed for institutional learning, executive workshops, and applied academic use.

Version 2.0 – Research-Enabled Edition

Deliverable Context

This simulation serves as an educational demonstrator within Deliverable 3.5, supporting applied training in strategic accessibility governance, inclusive digital transformation, and compliance-oriented decision-making.

It is designed to illustrate multi-dimensional impact interactions across policy, implementation, procurement, legal exposure, and stakeholder trust domains.

Indicators

Accessibility Index: higher values indicate stronger institutional accessibility performance.

Legal Risk: higher values indicate greater regulatory and compliance exposure.

Stakeholder Trust: higher values indicate stronger confidence among users, partners, and communities.

Technical Debt: higher values indicate accumulated implementation burden and fragility.

Budget: higher values indicate more financial flexibility for governance action.

The platform includes structured session logging, timestamped decision tracking, anonymized dataset export, and CSV output functionality to support academic research and institutional analysis.

This simulation model was developed for research and educational use in strategic accessibility governance contexts.

How to Play

You are acting as an accessibility advisor within a strategic governance environment.

Each round presents one governance decision. Indicators update dynamically, hidden effects reveal delayed consequences, and drift rules model systemic interactions between policy choices and institutional conditions.

How to Read Indicator Status

Each indicator card includes a status marker. These markers help participants interpret risk quickly and support users who do not rely on color perception.

Good indicates a relatively stable condition. Watch indicates a developing pressure that should be monitored. Critical indicates a severe condition that may contribute to institutional failure.

The simulation ends after 20 rounds or earlier if a failure threshold is reached.

Learning Impact

Participants develop applied WCAG reasoning at governance level, trade-off analysis, systems thinking, legal compliance awareness, participatory design understanding, and long-term technical debt awareness.

This edition references WCAG principles, EN 301 549, Universal Design, the Social Model of Disability, and participatory governance models.
