About the Lab

Inclusive Decision Lab is a strategic accessibility governance simulation framework designed for institutional learning, executive workshops, and applied academic use.

Version 1.0

Deliverable Context

This simulation serves as an educational demonstrator within Deliverable 3.5, supporting applied training in strategic accessibility governance, inclusive digital transformation, and compliance-oriented decision-making.

It is designed to illustrate multi-dimensional impact interactions across policy, implementation, procurement, legal exposure, and stakeholder trust domains.

Indicators

The simulation uses five indicators to track the consequences of your choices over time. These indicators are not separate mini-scores. Together, they show the overall condition of the system you are managing, and they are used at the end of the simulation to evaluate how balanced, resilient, and sustainable your decisions were.

Accessibility Index: shows how strong the organisation's accessibility performance is. Higher values usually mean fewer barriers across services.

Legal Risk: shows how exposed the organisation is to complaints, non-compliance, or regulatory action. Higher values mean more risk.

Stakeholder Trust: shows how much confidence users, partners, and affected communities have in the organisation. Higher values mean stronger trust.

Technical Debt: shows how much implementation burden, fragility, or future repair work is building up. Higher values usually mean the system is becoming harder to maintain well.

Budget: shows how much financial room remains for corrective action and strategic investment. Higher values mean more flexibility.

As you answer each scenario, these indicators move up or down. At the end of the simulation, your final profile across all five indicators contributes to your overall evaluation, feedback, and strategic maturity result.

The platform includes structured session logging, timestamped decision tracking, anonymized dataset export, and CSV output functionality to support academic research and institutional analysis.

Participants develop applied WCAG reasoning at governance level, trade-off analysis, systems thinking, legal compliance awareness, participatory design understanding, and long-term technical debt awareness.

Drift & Interaction Logic

In this simulation, one decision can affect more than one indicator at the same time. The indicators also influence each other over time, so a problem in one area can create pressure somewhere else.

If accessibility goes down, legal risk may go up. If trust goes down, governance becomes harder because confidence in the organisation weakens. If the budget becomes constrained, technical debt may increase because short-term fixes often replace stronger long-term work.

This means the simulation is not only asking whether a decision helps one area immediately. It is also asking whether that decision creates wider consequences across the whole system.

How to Play

You take the role of an accessibility advisor working inside a complex organisation.

In each round, you respond to one strategic scenario. Your choice affects the system indicators, may trigger hidden downstream effects, and can create knock-on pressure in other areas.

Step-by-Step Flow

  1. Read the category, title, and description in the main scenario panel.
  2. Open the Decision Dashboard if you want to check the current indicator values before deciding.
  3. Review the available response options and choose one.
  4. After you answer, stay on the same scenario and read the feedback, recommended answer, and hidden effect.
  5. When you are ready, select Next Question to move forward.
  6. Repeat this process until you complete all 20 scenarios or the system reaches a failure threshold earlier.

What the Indicators Do

The indicators are the main way the simulation tracks the impact of your decisions. They show whether your choices are strengthening the system, shifting pressure somewhere else, or creating longer-term risk.

You are not trying to maximise only one indicator. A strong result usually comes from keeping the full set of indicators in a workable balance.

How to Read Indicator Status

Each indicator card includes a status marker. These markers help participants interpret risk quickly and support users who do not rely on color perception.

Good indicates a relatively stable condition. Watch indicates a developing pressure that should be monitored. Critical indicates a severe condition that may contribute to institutional failure.

How to Use the Dashboard

The Decision Dashboard helps you review the current state of the organisation before making a choice. Open the dashboard from the left-side control whenever you want to compare the five indicators.

Use it to see which areas are stable, which areas need attention, and which areas are close to becoming critical. This helps you judge whether a decision is improving the whole system or simply moving the problem elsewhere.

What Happens After You Answer

Once you choose an option, the simulation updates the indicators immediately and shows you what changed. It also reveals a short strategic reflection, the recommended answer for that scenario, and any hidden effect tied to your decision.

This means you can pause, read the outcome, and understand the reasoning before moving on to the next question.

How the Final Result Is Judged

At the end of the simulation, the platform reviews your overall pattern of decisions across all five indicators. You then receive a final score, comments, rationale, and advice based on the condition of the system you created.

The simulation ends after 20 rounds or earlier if a failure threshold is reached.
